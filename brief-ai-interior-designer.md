# Design Brief — "AI Interior Designer"

## The one-line objective
Design a high-fidelity homepage and connected product-experience concept for a premium virtual interior design platform. A homeowner should come away believing: *"I can upload my real rooms, define my style, and receive beautiful, designer-quality transformations, mood boards, palettes, and shopping guidance tailored to my actual home."*

## Your role
You are the design director on this project. I've given you the goal and the guardrails below. **You decide** the homepage structure, section order, layout, visual system, and how features are combined and foregrounded. Where I haven't specified something, make the most elegant choice and keep moving — don't ask, decide.

---

## Tier 1 — Fixed (non-negotiable)

**The feeling it must land:** working with a high-end interior designer, without the consultant. Personal, elevated, deeply useful. The AI should feel invisible and magical — the value is the dream home, never the technology.

**Positioning:** a luxury design studio meets a modern home concierge. Reference vector: *Architectural Digest* + one top-tier residential design firm. Pick the firm; don't average across ten influences.

**Capabilities that must be represented somewhere in the experience** (you decide where, how, and in what combination):
create a home project · property/address context · style direction · room-by-room organization · real-photo upload · room dimensions & spatial notes · AI redesign concepts layered over the user's own room photos · design recommendations (layout, lighting, materials, color, styling) · mood boards · color & finish palettes · curated furniture/shopping guidance · save, compare, refine.

**The one section that must be unmistakable:** the AI redesign moment. The user must instantly read *"that's my real room → here's what it could become,"* rendered with designer-grade realism (before/after or layered preview — your call).

**Hard must-nots:**
generic startup/SaaS look · dashboard-heavy layouts · sterile minimalism with no warmth · cheap shopping-marketplace UI · cartoon or sci-fi AI motifs · robotic copy · any screen that emphasizes technology over emotional value.

---

## Tier 2 — Open (your discretion)

- **Information architecture & section count** — build whatever narrative sells aspiration *and* capability. Merge, split, or reorder freely.
- **Layout & composition** — spacious, editorial, warm. Desktop-first, thoughtful mobile.
- **Visual system** — warm luxury neutrals anchored by one deep tone (walnut/espresso). You define the exact palette; don't try to honor a list of twelve.
- **Typography** — an elegant serif/display for headlines, a refined sans for body/UI. No default startup fonts.
- **Materiality & imagery** — photoreal interiors, believable transformations, real textures (oak, stone, linen, plaster, brass, bouclé, travertine).
- **Copy** — elevated, calm, design-literate. Never technical.

---

## Self-check before you finish
1. Would any screen fit in the print edition of *Architectural Digest*? If a screen looks like a generic startup, redo it.
2. Is the "my real room → its potential" moment the emotional peak of the page?
3. Have I made real design decisions, or just filled in blanks? Show taste, not compliance.

## Deliverable
A presentation-ready, brand-worthy homepage plus connected product-experience concepts that read as one premium consumer brand and one sophisticated design service.
