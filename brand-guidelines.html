01

The idea

AI Interior Designer speaks the way quiet luxury real estate speaks: few words, extreme scale, warmth over brightness, and emptiness treated as the most expensive material on the page. The intelligence is implied by the restraint — the technology never announces itself.

Pillar 01

Confident quiet

Small type in a large frame signals certainty. We never fill space to prove value; we leave it open to prove taste.

Pillar 02

Warmth as light

Every surface is lit like the golden hour. No pure white, no pure black, no cold blue — the palette is candlelit, not fluorescent.

Pillar 03

Editorial logic

Every screen composes like a magazine page: one image, one headline, one whisper. Never more than three text elements in view.

02

Wordmark

AI Interior Designer
Intelligent Spaces, Composed
AiD
Monogram

The primary wordmark sets the name in the display serif with Designer italicised — the single-word-emphasis convention carried through every headline in the system. The descriptor line beneath is set in the label style: small caps, brass, +34% tracking. The monogram encloses AiD in a hairline square for favicons, watermarks and photo corners — the lowercase italic i is the only place the "AI" is allowed to wink.

  • Never set the wordmark in bold, or in the sans-serif.
  • Never place it over busy photography — only in negative space or on a flat field.
  • Never pair it with tech iconography: no sparkles, robots, or gradient-purple "AI" cues.
  • Clearspace: one cap-height of the wordmark on all sides, minimum.

04

Typography

Two voices only: a high-contrast serif that speaks, and a tracked-out sans that whispers. There are no middle sizes — the scale jumps by design, roughly 8:1 from display to caption.

Rule of emphasis

One italic word per headline, maximum. The italic marks the emotional centre of the sentence — composed, listen, home — and is the only emphasis device permitted. No bold, no colour changes mid-sentence, no underlines.

Rule of three

No composition carries more than three text elements: an eyebrow, a headline, a support line. If a fourth is needed, the layout is wrong — split the section.

06

Imagery

Whether photographed or AI-rendered, every image obeys the same light: golden hour or dusk, warm grade, lifted shadows. Renders should be indistinguishable from architectural photography — never glossy, never obviously synthetic.

Golden hour — hero exteriors
Dusk — dark-mode grounds
Linen daylight — editorial insets
  • Warmth onlyNo saturated blues, no cold daylight, no clinical render lighting. Skies appear at dusk or not at all.
  • Negative spaceEvery hero image must contain a quiet zone large enough to hold a headline. Compose (or generate) with the type in mind.
  • Sparse stylingLinen, oak, stone, a single object per surface. If a render feels furnished, remove a third of it.
  • People, barelyFigures appear small-in-frame, turned away, or cropped. Never posed at camera, never smiling at the lens.
  • UnderexposeGrade half a stop darker than natural. The system's darkness is where its type lives.
07

Interface & motion

Interaction follows the same restraint: hairlines instead of boxes, underlines instead of fills, and motion that behaves like slow air — nothing bounces, nothing pops.

Buttons — Light Explore the studio

Image hover: scale 1.03 over 800ms. Hover the frame above.

Buttons — Dark View the journal

Primary actions are outlined rectangles; secondary actions are tracked text links with a brass underline that draws in on hover. No filled pills, no drop shadows, no rounded corners anywhere in the system.

Timing

Entrances fade and rise 24px over 700ms ease-out (as the sections of this document do). Hovers respond in 350–450ms. Page transitions cross-fade through charcoal, never white.

Never

No bounce or spring easing, no parallax deeper than 10%, no auto-playing carousels, no skeleton shimmer — loading states use Principle VIII instead.

08

Voice

Declarative, short, unhurried. Sell the feeling, never the feature. The word "AI" appears in the name and almost nowhere else — intelligence is demonstrated, not advertised. No exclamation points, ever.
